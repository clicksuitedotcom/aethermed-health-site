Preparing Medical Records for Treatment in China

Clear records help doctors understand your case faster. For international patients, record preparation is often the difference between a general answer and a meaningful specialist review.

This guide is for general education and care-coordination planning. It is not medical advice, diagnosis, emergency guidance, or a substitute for a licensed physician.

Start with a one-page summary

Prepare a concise summary that includes the diagnosis, date of diagnosis, major treatments, current symptoms, current medications, allergies, other medical conditions, and your main goals for China-based review or treatment.

Collect the core documents

  • Diagnosis reports, discharge summaries, and operation notes.
  • Recent lab results, pathology reports, and imaging reports.
  • Original imaging files when available, not only screenshots or summaries.
  • Medication list with dosage, frequency, and start date.
  • Prior treatment timeline, including chemotherapy, radiation, surgeries, rehabilitation, or procedures.
  • Insurance documents if payment support or reimbursement documentation may be needed.

Translation and formatting

Chinese hospitals may review English records in some international departments, but bilingual summaries often reduce confusion. Keep filenames simple, organize documents by date, and separate imaging, labs, pathology, treatment notes, and administrative documents.

Questions to include

List three to five specific questions. For example: Is the diagnosis complete? Are there alternative treatment options? Is surgery feasible? What additional tests are required? How long might the process take?
